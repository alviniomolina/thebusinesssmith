Challenger : Organising events
The dutch weather

Starting as a young cultural entrepeneur at the age of 26, I had no clue I would enter maybe the best business learning environment there is. Organizing anual festivals had me working 9 months for one day of succes or failure. Succes or failure was almost 80% dependant on th weather, rain meant no poeople, sun meant no people, lightly clouded meant we are in business.

While doing my cultural events, I managed to get some of my risks coverd by ussage of subsidies en funding. I wrote a solid business (cultural) plan with my product and costs estimatites and woudl send it to about 8 different parties. Even if you started your preces in time, still you would have to wait months for response if it was granted. So each prositive or negative repsonse would required me to adjust my final festival without letting go of quality and quantity. This meant I had to be really agile and do some har negotiations to get the job done for sometimes 50% of the initial costs calculations.

Lessons learned
I learned to deal with failure caused with external events
I learned how to enticipate ans still do well in de worst case scenario.
I learned how to build modulair, having different scenario's that could adapt to any unforseen incident.
